Collapse each node into a morphing graph bubble

Decisionkey

Each node starts as a big kind-coloured icon face with its title beside it, and clicking morphs it into the full card.

WHY

Animating border-radius, fill and padding on a single element (rather than swapping two elements) keeps the open and closed states reading as one object resizing, with no layout jump.

Showing the bubble raised the question of how it sits on the spine.

One marker, drawn in front of the lines

Principle

The kind icon is the only marker in both states; the separate spine bead was removed, and the bubble is lifted above the connecting lines.

WHY

Two circles (a bead plus a badge) read as clutter and an accidental duplicate when a card opened. Collapsing to one marker, with the spine tucked behind it via z-index, makes the graph legible.

Finally, a single repo holds many conversations, so reports needed history.

Give each conversation its own report, linked from a master…

Decisionkey

Reports are written per conversation under .goldy/reports/, and a master index page links to every one, newest first.

WHY

A repo accumulates many sessions. Overwriting one file would lose the history, so each session is its own report and a manifest (index.json) drives a hub page that ties them together.

In summary: a friendlier, navigable report

Summarykey

The rework left the report easier to read and to keep:
